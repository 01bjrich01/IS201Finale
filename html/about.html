What is Weightlifting?

Don't worry, we'll keep it short.

Weight lifting, also known as resistance training, involves using weights to challenge your muscles. This can be done with free weights like dumbbells, weight machines, or even your own body weight. The goal is to increase muscle strength, endurance, and size by progressively lifting heavier weights.

Why lift weights? Besides making you strong enough to carry all your groceries in one trip, it has numerous health benefits. Weight lifting improves bone density, boosts metabolism, enhances mood, and reduces the risk of chronic diseases. Plus, it’s a great way to impress friends with your newfound strength and stamina.

Weight lifting can be fun too! It’s about celebrating small victories, like graduating from "baby weights" to "look, ma, I have muscles!" You’ll enjoy the camaraderie at the gym and the pride in your progress. Even if you never win a tug-of-war with a bear, you'll feel great knowing you could at least give it a decent challenge. So grab those weights and start lifting—because strong is the new sexy, and your future self will thank you for it!
